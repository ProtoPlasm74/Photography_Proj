iPhone SE

The iPhone SE: giant in 2012, tiny in 2014, and band new in 2016. Yes, this gem was released in 2016 with current generation(iPhone 6s) specs in a case that is legacy. The best thing is that this is considered a "low end" phone at $500 for the 64gig storage version, but this is a high end smartphone with, arguebly, the best iPhone design to date. There is no competition in the small phone market with the iPhone SE. However, if you enjoy big phones at a premium, the iPhone 6s and 7 have competition. *cough* Google Pixel and OnePlus 3T *cough*

Samson Go Mic

Samson may be an unfamilar brand to some, but they deal with high end audio equipment. With the Samson Go Mic, it becomes an affordable option at $40. This is competing with the popular Blu Yeti Snowball mic in price range(Trine even uses the Snowball in the Mac lab), but as for sound quality, there is no competition with the Samson Go Mic. After some rerearch, the Samson Go Mic blows the Snowball away in terms of portability, audio quality, build quaility, and options availiable such switching betweeen polaroid, cardiod, and -10DB. The only thing that is disappointing for some is that it is a usb mic, but the performance is more than enough to justify the $40 barrier. This mic is for people looking to have a better recording experience for a low price.
