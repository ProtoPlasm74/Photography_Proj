Tech that I use on a daily basis - Reviewed

iPhone SE

The iPhone SE: giant in 2012, tiny in 2014, and band new in 2016. Yes, this gem was released in 2016 with current generation(iPhone 6s) specs in a case that is legacy. The best thing is that this is considered a "low end" phone at $500 for the 64gig storage version, but this is a high end smartphone with, arguebly, the best iPhone design to date. There is no competition in the small phone market with the iPhone SE. However, if you enjoy big phones at a premium, the iPhone 6s and 7 have competition. *cough* Google Pixel and OnePlus 3T *cough* Displayed is the gold version with a marble and leather dbrand skin. This is a companion that never leaves me.

Samson Go Mic

Samson may be an unfamilar brand to some, but they deal with high end audio equipment. With the Samson Go Mic, it becomes an affordable option at $40. This is competing with the popular Blu Yeti Snowball mic in price range(Trine even uses the Snowball in the Mac lab), but as for sound quality, there is no competition with the Samson Go Mic. After some rerearch, the Samson Go Mic blows the Snowball away in terms of portability, audio quality, build quaility, and options availiable such switching betweeen polaroid, cardiod, and -10DB. The only thing that is disappointing for some is that it is a usb mic, but the performance is more than enough to justify the $40 barrier. This mic is for people looking to have a better recording experience for a low price.

Audio Technica M50x

These cans are bosses. I purchased these puppies at $120, which was beyond worth it. The performance is outstanding if you are looking for a true to sound experience(sorry bass heads). I use these for plugging into my amp for playing electric guitar in my dorm. I also enjoy listening to music for entertainment too. The "x" in the model means that you can change the cord(3ft, 10ft, and coiled 6ft). The 10ft cable is perfect for jam sessions, the 3ft is good for listening on my laptop, and the coiled is awesome for my desktop experience. The look of these are pretty nice, but I skinned it much like the iPhone SE. They are quite large, so I would not recommed wearing these in public often. If you're a wannabe audiophile like me, then you need these.

Clarion XC1410 with 4x Kicker 40CS654 Speakers

So you got a new car, so the first thing you do, naturally, is amp it up! At least that's how my mind works. Disclamer: I am not a bass head; I enjoy solid low-end with beautiful highs. This system displayed here gets very loud with supurb audio quality, and the stock system and stock look. Coming in at $200 for 4 speakers with built in tweeters and a 4 channel amp to power them, it was a good buy. You will have to spend some time and research intalling the system, but I managed to do it in an afternoon. This seemingly subtle change to my car transformed my Honda Civic into a party bus. Although I don't get sponcered...Crutchfield is a very good source for this kind of stuff!

Sony Cyber-Shot DSC-WX300

Most of the photos that are displayed(exept for these photos of course) were taken with this camera. While it is no DSLR, it gets the job done. The best thing about it is the zoom; it has an impressive 20x optical zoom and 40x digital zoom! It is the smallest camera with the best optical zoom that you can buy. I got it for less than $100 used on eBay, and I am pretty satified. The iPhone SE takes pretty comparable photos, but there is no competition when it comes to zoom and video stabilization. It also comes with a bunch of features like WiFi, Panarama mode, night mode, and a lot more. A downside of this camera is the lack of manual controls. The auto features are pretty solid, but sometimes a little more control is desired. I try to carry this camera wherever I go because of its portability: it fits in a pocket with ease. Most people will not need this, but it is nice to have for those better-than-smartphone shots.

Pebble Steel...Discontinued as of 12/7/2016 :(

The Pebble Steel...being an original backer of the Pebble, this tugs at the heart a bit. This would have been the most recommended item on this list, but Pebble got purchased by Fitbit just a few days ago just to get rid of the competition. With big companies like Apple and Google, Pebble could not stay afloat. The Pebble was the first smartwatch and the only smartwatch that did it right to this day: it was a watch first. With a 7 day battery life and an always-on display, there is nothing else like it. Apple and Google have smartphones on a wrist with a worse than smartphone battery, and Fitbit is just a band that often gets tossed aside after the motivation to be fit subsides. Did I mention the Pebble was waterproof up to 50 meters with it's first generation? I cannot recommed this piece of tech due to its sad ending. I will be using mine until it dies, then I will be going back to a dumb-watch unless other campanies make something that can come close to the Pebble.
R.I.P. Pebble...many will never know you
